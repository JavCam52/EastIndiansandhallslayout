Rationale

Our group chose to explore the topic "East Indians and the Settlement of Hall’s Layout" to shed light on an often overlooked aspect of Belizean history. This topic is significant because it highlights the contributions, challenges, and cultural legacy of East Indian communities in the development of Belize, particularly in Corozal.

While much historical focus has been placed on the African and Maya populations in Belize, we felt it was important to research and document the experiences of East Indians who also played a vital role in shaping the country's multicultural identity.

The settlement of Hall’s Layout, established by George Hall, a descendant of Indian indentured laborers, is a tangible symbol of East Indian heritage and community building in Belize. Through this research, we aimed to explore not only historical migration patterns but also social integration and economic impact, as illustrated by the Hall family’s story.

We selected a combination of sources, visual materials, oral histories, and documentary evidence to create a well-rounded understanding of our topic. A personal interview with Mr. Steven Hall, grandson of George Hall, added valuable insights into the family legacy and the enduring significance of Hall’s Layout in Corozal.
